Let's talk movies, specifically five iconic Studio Ghibili ones!

1. THE SECRET WORLD OF ARRIETTY The Secret World of Arrietty, released in Japan in 2010 takes place in a family home. It was based on the 1952 novel The Borrowers by Mary Norton. In this film Arrietty's family, survive by taking small things from the human home they secretly inhabit. When the family spots them, how will their live's change?
2. SPIRITED AWAY The incredible Spirited Away, directed and written by Hayao Miyazaki, was released in 2001. The film went on to become the highest-grossing film in Japanese history. Spirited Away features a teenage girl heroine who gets lost in an abandoned amusment park. Some main themes are identity, isolation, loss and empowerment.
3. THE WIND RISES The Wind Rises is the 2013 tale of young protangonist Jiro Horikoshi's dream to become a pilot. The film was supposed to be released simultaneously with The Tale of the Princess Kaguya, another Ghibli film, but in the end The Tale of the Princess Kaguya's realease date was delayed.
4. MY NEIGHBOR TOTORO My Neighbor Totoro is a Ghibili classic for a reason. The fairly old, 1988 movie follows two young girls named Satsuki and Mei along their journey with friendly wood spirits. The first meeting with Totoro happens one rainy night while the girls wait for the bus and all of a sudden Totoro appears beside them.
5. HOWL'S MOVING CASTLE Howl's Moving Castle is an amazing fantasy Ghibili film, released in 2004. The general premise of the film is based on the Howl's Moving Castle 1986 novel by Diana Wynne Jones. The plot follows a young girl named Sophie on the adventure to break the curse inflicted on her, after she was turned into a 90 year old women.
